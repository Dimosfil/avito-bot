# Avito Market Positioning Notes

Last updated: 2026-06-30.

These notes capture compact competitive observations for packaging Module 2 on
Avito. They are not raw scraped data; they preserve positioning lessons that
should guide the project's own listing, sales copy, and visual assets.

## Our Current Listing Baseline

Source: `https://www.avito.ru/voronezh/predlozheniya_uslug/ai-boty_dlya_biznesa._telegram_max_avito_crm_8217351862`

- Current offer: AI bots for business across Telegram, MAX, Avito, CRM.
- Visible price: 5,000 RUB.
- Strength: broader channel promise than Telegram-only competitors.
- Weakness: one generic robot image does not explain the business outcome.
- Weakness: description contains SEO-like keyword stuffing and mixed Cyrillic /
  Latin lookalike letters, which can reduce buyer trust.
- Packaging direction: sell incoming lead handling and manager handoff rather
  than a generic bot.

## Competitor: Telegram Bot Under Key

Source: `https://www.avito.ru/voronezh/predlozheniya_uslug/sozdam_telegram-bota_pod_klyuch_dlya_biznesa_8046377079`

- Offer: Telegram bot under key for business.
- Visible price: 5,000 RUB.
- Seller signal: named seller, shop profile, custom avatar, 2+ years and 200+
  projects claims in visual packaging.
- Strong packaging pattern: title, price, gallery, and copy all point to one
  simple category: Telegram bot.
- Gallery pattern: 5 images function as a mini-deck: main offer, order/payment
  scenario, integrations, work categories, trust/experience.
- Copy pattern: first line states practical outcomes: applications, sales,
  support 24/7; then sections for what is built, terms, who it suits, about the
  seller, and a direct CTA.
- Weakness: uses mixed Cyrillic / Latin lookalike letters and broad claims such
  as "any complexity", which can look spammy.
- Lesson for this project: match or exceed the gallery structure, but position
  broader than Telegram by showing Avito, website, VK/MAX, CRM/table, AI reply,
  and manager handoff.

## Competitor: AI Agents And Business Process Automation

Source: `https://www.avito.ru/voronezh/predlozheniya_uslug/ii-agenty_i_avtomatizatsiya_biznes-protsessov_8194588758`

- Offer: AI agents and business process automation.
- Visible price: from 10,000 RUB.
- Seller signal: individual seller Danil, Avito since July 2017, 7 active
  listings, standard avatar, phone visible, reply time several hours.
- Visual pattern: one strong cover image with large text about AI agents and
  business process automation, plus a small-business opportunity subline. The
  image shows a workshop/production context, dashboard overlays, FastAPI,
  DeepSeek, Python automation, and auto lead intake enabled.
- Copy pattern: sells higher-level business automation, not only chat bots.
  Mentions AI assistant for applications, bot consultant on company materials,
  sales department automation, call/chat analysis, routine process automation,
  integrations, and internal tools.
- Strong strategic idea: uses "AI agent" language to justify a higher starting
  price and broader scope than simple bot listings.
- Strong buyer framing: says the seller first analyzes what can realistically
  be automated, proposes solution scheme, stages, timeline, budget, starts from
  a small pilot, then scales after testing.
- Weakness: only one image, no mini-deck gallery.
- Weakness: SEO tail and mixed Cyrillic / Latin lookalike letters reduce trust.
- Weakness: very broad scope can feel less concrete than a lead-processing
  product.
- Lesson for this project: borrow the "pilot then scale" and "realistic
  automation audit" framing, but keep the offer more concrete: first-line lead
  handling from Avito and other channels, AI replies from a knowledge base,
  handoff intent detection, CRM/table logging, manager notification, and manual
  takeover.

## External Marketplace Reference: Kwork AI Agent / LLM Integration

Source: `https://kwork.ru/script-programming/50203350/ai-agent-i-llm-gpt-integratsiya-v-vash-proekt-openai-claude-ii-chat-bot`

- Offer: AI agent and LLM/GPT integration into an existing project; mentions
  OpenAI, Claude, and AI chatbot in the listing title.
- Visible price in page title: 5,000 RUB.
- Seller signal in title: named seller Emil Rokossovsky, username `rokoss21`.
- Marketplace proof should be checked manually in the live Kwork UI before it
  is reused in Avito copy; static HTML did not reliably expose review/order
  counts during this study.
- Cover image pattern: minimal expert-brand cover with a person photo, dark
  background, and short promise about implementing an AI agent, bot, or business
  assistant.
- Strong strategic idea: packages "AI agent / LLM integration" as an entry
  service at 5,000 RUB, which helps buyers understand that the first step can be
  small even when the larger concept sounds advanced.
- Limitation of observation: Kwork did not expose the full listing description
  in static HTML during this study; conclusions are based on visible title,
  cover image, seller/profile signals, and search/category snippets.
- Lesson for this project: Avito copy can safely use an entry-service framing:
  start with a small pilot or first integration, then expand to a fuller AI
  assistant/agent after the buyer sees a working scenario.
- Lesson for this project: add concise technology credibility only after the
  business outcome. Mention providers or LLMs as "OpenAI/Claude/DeepSeek or
  another suitable model" only if relevant to the buyer, not as the headline.

## Positioning Rules For Our Avito Packaging

- Lead with a concrete business result: AI bot for incoming leads from Avito,
  Telegram, VK/MAX, and website, rather than AI bots in the abstract.
- Make manager handoff a first-class differentiator: the bot answers first,
  qualifies the client, detects intent such as quotation/deal requests, and
  calls a manager with the dialogue context.
- Use plain trustworthy wording. Avoid keyword stuffing and mixed lookalike
  letters in public copy.
- Build a 5-7 image gallery before buying promotion: cover offer, lead flow,
  integrations, AI knowledge-base replies, manager handoff, packages/pricing,
  launch process.
- Price ladder should separate simple bot, business workflow bot, and AI
  assistant/agent so the 5,000 RUB entry price does not conflict with a more
  expensive AI promise.
- Use small pilot first as the risk reducer for buyers: define one channel, one
  lead scenario, one manager handoff path, then scale after test.
- Profile trust matters: use a non-default avatar/logo, clear seller identity,
  and proof-like visuals instead of generic robot art.
